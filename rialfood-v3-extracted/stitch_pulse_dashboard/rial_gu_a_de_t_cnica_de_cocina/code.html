Why Sous-vide?

Traditional cooking methods like searing or roasting rely on high heat to transfer energy to the core of your food. This often results in an overcooked exterior and a narrow "sweet spot" in the center. Sous-vide (French for "under vacuum") uses a precise water bath to bring your protein to the exact desired temperature throughout, ensuring 100% consistency.

lightbulb Pro Tip

"Always pat your protein bone-dry before the final sear. Moisture is the enemy of the Maillard reaction. If the surface is wet, you'll steam the meat instead of crusting it."

Step-by-Step Instructions

Step 01

Preparation & Vacuum Sealing

Season your protein generously with salt and aromatics (thyme, rosemary, or crushed garlic). Place inside a BPA-free vacuum bag. If you don't have a vacuum sealer, use the water displacement method with a high-quality freezer bag.

Step 02

The Precision Bath

Set your immersion circulator to the target temperature. For a medium-rare steak, we recommend 54.5°C (130°F). Once the water reaches temperature, submerge the bag completely. Ensure no part of the bag is blocking the circulator's intake.

Step 03

The Finishing Sear

Remove the protein and pat dry. Heat a cast-iron skillet until smoking hot with a high-smoke-point oil (like avocado or grapeseed). Sear for only 45-60 seconds per side. Add butter and herbs in the last 30 seconds for a classic basting finish.
